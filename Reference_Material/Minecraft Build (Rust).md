Building the City of Rust on a massive Minecraft scale is going to be an incredible project, especially with WorldEdit to help shape the terrain and massive cylinder walls. Blending the grim, smog-choked atmosphere of 1st Industrial Revolution London with the dieselpunk, heavily stratified layout of Rust gives you a phenomenal aesthetic to work with.

Here is a breakdown of how to bring Rust to life in Minecraft, perfectly tailored for your TTRPG campaign.

### 1. Architectural Style

The architecture should reflect the massive wealth disparity and the heavy, mechanical nature of the city, structurally divided by those massive containment walls.

- **The Outer Slums & Working Class Rings:** Claustrophobic, cramped, and haphazard. Buildings should be narrow, towering Victorian-style tenements leaning against each other. Think narrow alleyways, suspended clotheslines (using string and banners), and exposed pipes.
    
- **The Factory & Merchant Ring:** The architecture here is brutalist and utilitarian. Massive square factories with towering cylindrical smokestacks that dominate the skyline. You will want large railway tracks, elevated subway lines, and heavy structural supports. The airship docks should jut out aggressively from the city walls.
    
- **The Elite Core (Palace Area):** Starkly contrasting the rest of the city. This area features pristine, opulent neo-Gothic architecture—sharp spires, large arched glass windows, and immaculate symmetry. It is surrounded by high-rise noble housing and encased within the visible dome of the anti-magic shield.
    
- **The Underground:** Carved directly into old mines. Jagged, unrefined cavern walls reinforced with heavy wooden beams and iron supports, housing the sprawling Black Market and hidden subway tunnels.
    

### 2. Flora, Fauna, and the Environment

Given the intense pollution and heavy industrialization, natural life is almost entirely choked out, creating a grim environment.

- **Flora:**
    
    - **Outside the Shield:** Dead, leafless trees (use stripped dark oak or spruce logs with no leaves). Patches of coarse dirt and podzol instead of grass. The only plant life should be resilient, invasive vines (weeping vines or regular vines) clinging to the damp brickwork of the slums.
        
    - **Inside the Shield:** A shocking burst of green. Manicured gardens, vibrant custom trees, and pristine grass blocks, highlighting the hoarding of clean resources by the elite.
        
- **Fauna:**
    
    - **The Streets:** Swarms of rats (represented by silverfish or custom heads), stray dogs (untamed wolves), and ravens/crows (parrots dyed black if using mods, or represented by bats flying in the smog).
        
    - **Beasts of Burden:** Heavy, smog-adapted horses pulling carts of coal and iron.
        

### 3. Typical Professions and Overall City Look

The city operates as a relentless machine, divided visually by a harsh color gradient.

- **The Look:** The outer edges of the city begin in drab, oppressive **grays** (ash and soot-covered). As you move inward toward the factory districts, the color palette shifts aggressively to harsh **reds** (baked bricks, rust, and furnace fires). Thick smoke (using campfires hidden in smokestacks or stained glass gradients) permanently hangs over the middle and outer rings.
    
- **Professions:**
    
    - **Lower Rings/Underground:** Coal/iron miners, scrap scavengers, Black Market smugglers, factory laborers, and subway operators.
        
    - **Middle Rings:** Steam-engineers, mechanics working on airships, mid-level merchants, military cadets (drilling near the military schools), and teachers at the Dawn School.
        
    - **Inner Core:** Politicians, Teleportation Company executives, high-ranking military officials, and magic-shield technicians.
        

### 4. Minecraft Block Palettes

These palettes are designed to work perfectly with WorldEdit gradient commands to create that gray-to-red transition.

**The Outer Slums (The Gray Zone)**

- **Primary:** Cobbled Deepslate, Tuff, Andesite, Gravel.
    
- **Accents:** Iron Bars, Cyan Terracotta (for faded, grim roofing), Spruce Planks (for rotting wood), Cobweb, Campfires.
    

**The Factory Ring (The Red/Rust Zone)**

- **Primary:** Bricks, Granite, Red Nether Bricks, Mud Bricks, Terracotta.
    
- **Accents:** Copper Blocks (heavily oxidized and regular), Cut Copper Stairs/Slabs, Iron Blocks, Chains, Anvils, Blast Furnaces.
    

**The Elite Core (The Clean Zone)**

- **Primary:** Smooth Quartz, Calcite, Polished Diorite, White Concrete.
    
- **Accents:** Dark Oak Planks (for rich contrast), Gold Blocks, Chiseled Stone Bricks, Light Blue Stained Glass (to build the massive anti-magic shield dome).
    

**The Underground/Mines**

- **Primary:** Stone, Cobblestone, Deepslate, Dirt Paths.
    
- **Accents:** Spruce Fences/Slabs (for supports), Lanterns, Redstone Torches, Rails.

### 5. Concept Art Prompts for Midjourney / DALL-E / Gemini

Use these prompts to generate visual references before you start laying down blocks:

**Prompt 1: The Macro View (The Gradient & The Walls)**

> Wide establishing shot of a massive, circular steampunk city named Rust, viewed from above. The city is segregated by massive, towering concrete walls into concentric rings. The outer rings are coated in gray ash and grim dieselpunk slums. The middle ring is dominated by brutalist red-brick factories, towering smokestacks emitting thick black pollution, and airships docked at iron platforms. In the very center, protected by a glowing, translucent blue anti-magic dome, is a pristine, opulent Victorian palace surrounded by clean high-rises. 80s London meets 90s Russia industrial aesthetic.

**Prompt 2: The Street Level (Factory District)**

> Eye-level perspective from a claustrophobic, dirty street in a 19th-century industrial steampunk city. The buildings are towering, oppressive red brick tenement houses and factories, connected by heavy iron pipes and elevated subway train tracks. The sky is completely blotted out by thick smog. Cadets from a military school march through the mud, while engineers in heavy leather gear work on brass machinery. Harsh orange light spills from furnace windows into the gloomy, soot-covered street.

**Prompt 3: The Underground Black Market**

> Concept art of a bustling, hidden black market built entirely inside a massive, abandoned subterranean mine. The cavern walls are jagged stone supported by thick wooden beams and rusty iron pillars. Makeshift stalls and tents are clustered around an old, underground subway track. The scene is illuminated by the eerie glow of scattered lanterns and sparking dieselpunk machinery. Rough, dangerous laborers and smugglers trade illicit goods in the shadows

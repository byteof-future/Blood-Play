# Initiative & Surprise (Tempo Mechanics)

The core flow of combat relies on who has the **Tempo** (the momentum of the fight).

### 1. Detection
If parties are unaware of each other, no combat occurs. You can safely maneuver or set up an ambush.

### 2. Spotting
If Party A spots Party B (who is unaware):
- **Option 1 - Ambush:** 
  Roll Stealth (Party A) vs Perception (Party B).
  - *Success:* Party A gains **TEMPO** (1 free action) -> Enters Surprise Round.
  - *Failure:* Proceed to standard Initiative.
- **Option 2 - Head-On:** 
  Roll Agility/Dexterity (Party A) vs Reflex (Party B).
  - *Success:* Party A gains **TEMPO** -> Standard Initiative.
  - *Failure:* Standard Initiative.

### 3. Surprise Round
The attacking party takes their Tempo action. The surprised party may use their Reaction to roll Reflex for defense, but they cannot take offensive actions.

### 4. Standard Initiative
Roll for standard turn order if no Tempo was triggered, or proceed to standard turn order after the Surprise Round concludes.

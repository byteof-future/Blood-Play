# Traits

**See Through** - Slimes are transparent or translucent, making them difficult to spot when motionless. Creatures have disadvantage on Perception checks to notice a slime that isn't moving. Slimes can hide in plain sight by staying still.

**Amorphous** - Slimes can squeeze through spaces as small as 1 inch wide. They cannot be grappled or restrained by physical means. Weapons that rely on precision (piercing, slashing) deal half damage to slimes.

**Acid Touch** - Any creature that touches the slime or hits it with a melee attack (within 5 feet) takes 1d4 acid damage. This is automatic and doesn't require an action from the slime.

**Sticky** - When a slime successfully grapples a creature, that creature is also stuck and has disadvantage on checks to escape. The slime can grapple creatures up to its own size.

# Skills and Abilities

## Skills
Skills can be used once per long rest

1. **Bounce** - The slime can launch itself up to 10 feet through the air to land on a target. If it hits (Reflex save DC 10), target takes 1d6 blunt damage and is grappled with +4 bonus to the grapple. Useful for reaching elevated targets or ambushing from above.

## Abilities
Abilities can be used 1 time per day (= Fight Level)

1. **Engulf** (requires grapple) - If the slime has grappled a Small or smaller creature, it can attempt to engulf them. Target must make Strength save (DC 10) or be pulled inside the slime. Engulfed creatures are blinded, restrained, take 1d6 acid damage at start of each turn, and cannot breathe. They can escape with a successful Strength check (DC 12) on their turn.

# Description

Slimes are among the most basic and ubiquitous monsters in the world, found in nearly every environment. These gelatinous oozes are living blobs of semi-transparent protoplasm that slowly ooze across surfaces, absorbing organic matter for sustenance. The Plains Variant is typically a pale green or clear color, allowing it to blend with grass and pooled water.

Despite their simple biology, slimes are surprisingly dangerous to the unwary. Their transparent bodies make them difficult to spot when they're lying in wait. They attack by extending pseudopods to strike and grapple prey, then slowly dissolve their catch with acidic secretions. Slimes have no eyes or brain as creatures understand them, but they can sense vibrations and chemical signatures, allowing them to "hunt" effectively.

Slimes reproduce by division - when a slime grows large enough (after consuming sufficient food), it splits into two smaller slimes. This makes them a persistent problem in certain areas, as killing one slime might mean two more appear weeks later.

**Size Categories:**
- **Young Slime:** 1-2 feet diameter, 21 CHP
- **Mature Slime:** 3-4 feet diameter, 40 CHP
- **Elder Slime:** 5+ feet diameter, 60 CHP (considered FL 2)

**Variants by Environment:**
- **Plains (Green):** Standard stats as above
- **Cave (Gray):** +5 Stealth in darkness, cold resistance
- **Swamp (Brown):** Disease carrier, creatures hitting it make RES save or poisoned
- **Dungeon (Blue):** Can climb walls and ceilings
- **Volcanic (Red):** Acid damage becomes fire damage, fire immunity

**Tactics:** Slimes have no real tactics - they simply move toward vibrations and attempt to engulf anything organic. They prefer to wait motionless near game trails or water sources, then attack when prey comes close. When injured, slimes don't flee; they fight until destroyed. Multiple slimes will converge on the same target without coordination.

**Habitat:** Anywhere damp and dark. Common in caves, sewers, dungeons, forests, swamps, and grasslands. They tend to gather near water sources or in areas with regular traffic (more food opportunities).

**Encounter Notes:** Slimes are classic low-level monsters, perfect for introducing new players to combat. The real danger is:
1. **Surprise:** Hard to spot when still
2. **Persistence:** They don't stop attacking
3. **Acid damage:** Degrades armor over time
4. **Numbers:** Where there's one slime, there are often more

A single Young Slime is appropriate for a level 1-2 party as a minor threat. Two or three together become a moderate challenge. Elder Slimes should be treated as mini-bosses for low-level parties.

**Interesting Uses:**
- Dungeon maintenance (cleaning corpses and waste)
- Biological traps (placed deliberately in corridors)
- Alchemy components (their acid and cores are valuable)
- Waste disposal in cities (some cities keep controlled slime populations in sewers)

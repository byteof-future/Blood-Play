# Traits

**Shadow Stalker** - The Bag Man can teleport from one shadow to another within 60 feet as a bonus action. Must have dim light or darkness at both origin and destination. When in shadows, has advantage on Stealth checks.

**Dimension Bag** - The Bag Man's burlap sack is a portal to a pocket dimension. Creatures or objects placed inside are trapped in an extradimensional space. The bag can hold up to 3 Medium creatures or equivalent mass. Creatures inside are incapacitated and cannot breathe.

**Silent Predator** - The Bag Man makes no sound when moving. Creatures have disadvantage on Perception checks to hear it. Does not provoke opportunity attacks when moving away from enemies.

# Skills and Abilities

## Skills
Skills can be used once per long rest

1. **Teleport** - Can teleport from 1 dark spot to another within 120 feet, regardless of line of sight. Can bring grabbed creatures with it. Does not require shadows for this enhanced teleport, only dim light or darker.

## Abilities
Abilities can be used 5 times per day (= Fight Level)

1. **Terror** - All creatures within 30 feet who fail Intimidation check (DC 16) are frightened and cannot attack for 1 turn. Frightened creatures can only move away or dodge. They can repeat the save at the end of each turn.

2. **Blade Hands** - The Bag Man's hands transform into blade-like appendages for 1 minute. All claw attacks deal an additional 1d6 slashing damage and attacks gain +2 to hit. Can still use Snatch ability while Blade Hands is active.

3. **Snatch** - Can grab and put up to 1 Medium creature in its bag (extradimensional space) which completely immobilizes them until they can make a Strength save (DC 15) to escape. They take 1d6 suffocation damage per turn inside. Multiple creatures can attempt to escape on their turns.

# Description

The Bag Man is an urban legend come to life—a gaunt, impossibly tall figure that lurks in alleyways and shadows. Standing over 7 feet tall with unnaturally long limbs, its body is wrapped in filthy, tattered rags. Its face, if it can be called that, is hidden beneath a burlap sack with two ragged eyeholes that leak an oily black substance. The creature carries a large, stained burlap bag that seems far larger on the inside than it should be.

The Bag Man hunts at night, targeting lone travelers, children, or anyone foolish enough to wander dark streets alone. It stalks its prey with supernatural patience, teleporting between shadows to get closer before striking. Victims are grabbed and stuffed into its bag, never to be seen again—at least, not alive.

The origins of the Bag Man are unclear. Some say it was once human, cursed for terrible crimes. Others claim it's a demon that feeds on fear. Whatever its nature, the Bag Man is intelligent enough to plan ambushes and cruel enough to toy with its victims.

**Tactics:** The Bag Man prefers ambush tactics. It uses its high Stealth to position itself in shadows, then strikes with Shadow Strike for maximum damage. If the first attack doesn't drop the target, it attempts to grapple and use Snatch to bag them. When outnumbered, it uses Terror to scatter enemies, then focuses on isolating one victim. It teleports away if reduced below 50% HP, dragging any bagged victims with it.

**Habitat:** Urban environments, dark alleyways, abandoned buildings, sewer systems, or anywhere humans gather at night. Rarely seen in wilderness.

**Encounter Notes:** The Bag Man is a FL 5 monster suitable for a party of 3-4 level 5-7 characters. The real danger is its ability to remove party members from combat via Snatch. The fight becomes a race to free allies before they suffocate. Smart parties spread out to prevent multiple characters being grabbed, and keep light sources ready to eliminate shadows.

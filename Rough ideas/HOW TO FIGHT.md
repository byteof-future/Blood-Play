- Bleed: 

Takes 1d4 dmg to WHP for 3 turns.(stacks only 2 times)

- Concealed:

 This weapon is easily hidden from view. Opponents have disadvantage on Perception checks to spot it.

- Disarm:

 On a hit, you can attempt to force the target to drop one item it is holding. The target makes a Strength or Dexterity save to avoid being disarmed.

- Grapple:

 On a hit, the target is Grappled (escape DC based on your Strength or the weapon's design).

- Non-Lethal: 

When you reduce a creature to 0 CHP with this weapon, you can choose to knock it out instead of killing it.

- Parry: 

If you are proficient, you can use your reaction to add your Proficiency Bonus to your ARN against one melee attack.

- Trip: 

On a hit, you can attempt to knock the target prone. The target makes a Strength or Reflex save to avoid being knocked prone.

- Versatile: 

This weapon can be used with one or two hands. Its damage die changes accordingly (e.g., 1d8 one-handed, 1d10 two-handed).
